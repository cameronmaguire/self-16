The Rationale for SELF

SELF Requirements

In a maritime based accident event, identification of the location of the data, the locale of the ‘block-box’ is often hampered by;

  • The depth of the sea,
  • The range of the black-box radio beacon through water,
  • The battery life once power is on permanently,
  • Mechanical or physical failure,
  • Remoteness of the location.

SELF Design

SELF-16 has been designed to be able to satisfy the ICAO GADSS SARPS

  • The requirement for an aeroplane in distress to autonomously transmit information, from which a position can be determined at least once every minute.
  • The requirement to provide a high probability of locating an accident site to within a 6 NM radius.
  • The requirement for the autonomous transmission of position information to be capable of transmitting the information in the event of total electrical power loss.

SELF Tracking

Our own research identified that once the (current) 30-day battery life has expired, the time to find the aircraft data recorders has an average of over 600 days.
The SELF® has adapted a standard industry asset tracking device that is widely used to track the position of Trailers, Cargo Containers, Heavy Construction Equipment, Generators, Boats/Barges and any other valuable mobile asset.

SELF Function

It functions by processing GPS satellite signals to obtain its position in terms of longitude and latitude and re-transmits this information over Globalstar’s Simplex Satellite Network.
In Standard Messaging Mode, the SELF® emails its position at regular power-on intervals that are programmed during the configuration process, and then returns to dormant airline mode. Cycle time for a message is around 2 minutes.
