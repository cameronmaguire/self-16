Sea Trials

At the recent International Conference on Flight Location & Data Recovery in Orlando, Florida, our Operations Director took the opportunity to carry out some sea trials for our prototype SELF device. As you can see, the deployment of the device went well about 4-miles offshore from Cape Canaveral in Florida. The objective was to track the SELF through the currents in that area of the Atlantic to see if it would wash-up on the Florida coast or maybe get caught in the Gulf stream and head back to the UK!

After a week in the sea, working perfectly and sending out its tracking email alerts every six hours, the device did wash up on the Florida coast at a lovely place called Ballard Pines about 35 miles south of the release point, having circulated about 50 miles around the ocean. The final washing-up point was Lat/Lng: 27.897874, -80.467675.
